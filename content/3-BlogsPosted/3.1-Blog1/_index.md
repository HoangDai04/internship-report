# STREAMLINING ACCESS TO POWERFUL DISASTER RECOVERY CAPABILITIES OF AWS

In this article, AWS explains how organizations can build and implement an effective **Disaster Recovery (DR)** strategy to ensure that systems can quickly recover from unexpected events such as hardware failures, power outages, or cyberattacks.

## Key Highlights

* **Disaster Recovery is more than just backups:** Backing up data is essential, but it is not sufficient. Organizations also need to prepare their infrastructure, applications, and recovery procedures.
* **Combining AWS Backup and AWS Elastic Disaster Recovery:** These services work together to protect critical data and enable rapid system recovery after a failure.
* **Reduced downtime:** Automating the recovery process helps restore services more quickly and minimizes business disruption.
* **Multiple Disaster Recovery strategies:** AWS supports several approaches with different cost and availability levels, including **Backup & Restore, Pilot Light, Warm Standby,** and **Multi-Site**.
* **Infrastructure automation:** Infrastructure as Code (IaC) enables organizations to recreate environments quickly, consistently, and reliably.
* **Cost optimization:** Businesses can select the Disaster Recovery strategy that best matches their recovery objectives and budget instead of overprovisioning resources.

## Key Takeaway

An effective Disaster Recovery plan should be designed **before** a disaster occurs, not after. AWS provides a wide range of services that help organizations balance recovery time, system availability, and operational costs, ultimately reducing the impact of unexpected failures and ensuring business continuity.
